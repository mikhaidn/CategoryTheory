# AI/ML Learning Resources

> Curated resources tailored for a professional SWE with CS masters background

## Core Statistics & Probability

### Books
- **"All of Statistics"** by Larry Wasserman
  - Perfect bridge from descriptive to inferential statistics
  - Rigorous but accessible given your math background
  - Covers MLE, hypothesis testing, Bayesian inference

- **"Bayesian Methods for Hackers"** by Cameron Davidson-Pilon
  - FREE online: https://github.com/CamDavidsonPilon/Probabilistic-Programming-and-Bayesian-Methods-for-Hackers
  - Python-based, practical approach
  - Great for building intuition

- **"Think Bayes"** by Allen Downey
  - FREE online: https://greenteapress.com/wp/think-bayes/
  - Computational approach to Bayesian statistics

### Interactive Resources
- **Seeing Theory**: https://seeing-theory.brown.edu/
  - Visual introduction to probability and statistics
  - Excellent for building intuition

- **3Blue1Brown - Bayes Theorem**: https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HZGCoVF3YvM
  - Visual, intuitive explanation

## Mathematics for ML

### Linear Algebra
- **"Introduction to Linear Algebra"** by Gilbert Strang
  - Refresher + depth needed for ML
  - Video lectures: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/18-06-linear-algebra-spring-2010/

- **3Blue1Brown - Essence of Linear Algebra**: https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDPD3MizzM2xVFitgF8hE_ab
  - 15-video series, geometric intuition
  - Watch before diving into theory

### Optimization
- **"Convex Optimization"** by Boyd & Vandenberghe
  - FREE PDF: https://web.stanford.edu/~boyd/cvxbook/
  - Chapters 1-5 are essential for ML
  - Your CS background will help with the math

- **Stanford CS229 Optimization Notes**
  - https://cs229.stanford.edu/
  - Practical optimization for ML

## Machine Learning Foundations

### Books
- **"Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning"** by Christopher Bishop
  - Comprehensive, mathematical
  - Bayesian perspective (connects to Phase 1!)
  - Reference book, not cover-to-cover

- **"The Elements of Statistical Learning"** by Hastie, Tibshirani, Friedman
  - FREE PDF: https://hastie.su.stanford.edu/ElemStatLearn/
  - Statistical perspective on ML
  - More mathematical than most

- **"Hands-On Machine Learning"** by Aurélien Géron (3rd edition)
  - Practical with scikit-learn and TensorFlow/PyTorch
  - Good balance of theory and practice
  - Excellent for learning by doing

### Courses
- **Stanford CS229 - Machine Learning** (Andrew Ng)
  - FREE: https://cs229.stanford.edu/
  - Lecture notes are gold
  - More mathematical than Coursera version

- **Fast.ai - Practical Deep Learning**
  - FREE: https://course.fast.ai/
  - Top-down teaching approach
  - Complements theory with practice

## Deep Learning

### Books
- **"Deep Learning"** by Goodfellow, Bengio, Courville
  - FREE online: https://www.deeplearningbook.org/
  - Comprehensive, mathematical
  - Part I (math foundations) is excellent review

- **"Deep Learning with PyTorch"** by Stevens, Antiga, Viehmann
  - Practical, well-written
  - Good for getting hands-on

- **"Build a Large Language Model (From Scratch)"** by Sebastian Raschka
  - 2024 book, very current
  - Implements GPT-like model from scratch
  - Perfect for your "learn by implementing" style

### Courses
- **Stanford CS231n - CNNs for Visual Recognition**
  - FREE: http://cs231n.stanford.edu/
  - Excellent lecture notes and assignments
  - Andrej Karpathy's lectures are legendary

- **Stanford CS224n - NLP with Deep Learning**
  - FREE: http://web.stanford.edu/class/cs224n/
  - Covers RNNs, Transformers, LLMs
  - Updated regularly with latest techniques

- **Stanford CS25 - Transformers United**
  - FREE: https://web.stanford.edu/class/cs25/
  - Deep dive into Transformers
  - Guest lectures from researchers

### Interactive
- **3Blue1Brown - Neural Networks**: https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PLZHQObOWTQDNU6R1_67000Dx_ZCJB-3pi
  - Visual explanation of backpropagation
  - Watch before implementing from scratch

- **Distill.pub**: https://distill.pub/
  - Interactive ML research articles
  - Excellent for building intuition
  - Covers attention, optimization, interpretability

## Modern AI & LLMs

### Papers (Essential Reading)
- **"Attention Is All You Need"** (Vaswani et al., 2017)
  - The Transformer paper
  - Must read, foundation of modern NLP

- **"BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers"** (Devlin et al., 2018)

- **"Language Models are Few-Shot Learners"** (Brown et al., 2020)
  - GPT-3 paper

- **"Training language models to follow instructions with human feedback"** (Ouyang et al., 2022)
  - InstructGPT / ChatGPT methodology

- **"LoRA: Low-Rank Adaptation of Large Language Models"** (Hu et al., 2021)
  - Essential for fine-tuning

### Guides & Blogs
- **"The Illustrated Transformer"** by Jay Alammar
  - https://jalammar.github.io/illustrated-transformer/
  - Visual guide to Transformers
  - Start here before reading the paper

- **Prompt Engineering Guide**
  - https://github.com/dair-ai/Prompt-Engineering-Guide
  - Comprehensive, regularly updated

- **Lilian Weng's Blog**: https://lilianweng.github.io/
  - In-depth posts on latest AI research
  - "LLM Powered Autonomous Agents" is must-read

### Courses
- **Deeplearning.AI Short Courses**: https://www.deeplearning.ai/short-courses/
  - FREE courses on LangChain, RAG, fine-tuning, agents
  - Taught by framework creators
  - 1-2 hours each, very practical

- **Full Stack Deep Learning**: https://fullstackdeeplearning.com/
  - FREE course
  - Covers MLOps and production ML

## MLOps & Production ML

### Books
- **"Designing Machine Learning Systems"** by Chip Huyen
  - Best book on ML system design
  - Written for practitioners
  - Covers entire ML lifecycle

- **"Machine Learning Design Patterns"** by Lakshmanan, Robinson, Munn
  - Design patterns specific to ML
  - Your architecture skills will shine here

- **"Reliable Machine Learning"** by Todd Underwood et al.
  - Applying SRE practices to ML
  - Google's approach to production ML

### Papers
- **"Hidden Technical Debt in Machine Learning Systems"** (Sculley et al., Google)
  - https://papers.nips.cc/paper/5656-hidden-technical-debt-in-machine-learning-systems
  - Must-read, explains why ML systems are hard

- **"Machine Learning: The High-Interest Credit Card of Technical Debt"**
  - Follow-up to above
  - Your SWE experience will resonate with this

### Blogs & Resources

- **Chip Huyen's Blog**: https://huyenchip.com/

## Learning Strategy

Given your background:
1. **Skim/skip**: Basic programming, intro calculus, basic Python
2. **Review quickly**: Linear algebra, probability basics
3. **Focus on**: Inferential/Bayesian stats, optimization, ML algorithms, deep learning, LLMs, MLOps
4. **Leverage**: Your SWE skills for projects, architecture, production ML
